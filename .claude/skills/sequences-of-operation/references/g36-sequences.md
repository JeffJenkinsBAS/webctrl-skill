# ASHRAE Guideline 36 — Detailed Sequences, FDD, and Alarm Suppression

Reference for writing or reviewing sequences that cite Guideline 36. Confirm the project's cited **edition** (2018, 2021, or 2024) and included **addenda/options** before assuming any specific behavior below applies — G36 is a guideline, not a single fixed spec, and engineers frequently select which options to incorporate ([ASHRAE Guideline 36-2024 summary](https://online.standard.no/nb/ashrae-guideline-36-2024-high-performance-sequences-of-operation-for-hvac-systems-includes-supporting-files)).

## 1. What G36 Covers and Doesn't

G36 (RP-1455, ASHRAE TC 1.4) provides standardized, peer-reviewed sequences for common airside equipment (2018) and waterside equipment (2021 expansion), intended to maximize energy efficiency, provide control stability, and enable real-time FDD ([txaee.org G36 presentation](https://txaee.org/images/meeting/042721/aee_april_2021___ashrae_guideline_36.pdf)). It covers sequences, hardwired point lists, and control diagrams. It does **not** specify control hardware, network protocol, cybersecurity, or graphics — those come from Guideline 13 and the project spec.

**2024 edition additions** relevant to sequence content ([Standard.no G36-2024 summary](https://online.standard.no/nb/ashrae-guideline-36-2024-high-performance-sequences-of-operation-for-hvac-systems-includes-supporting-files); [ASHRAE Standards Actions March 2024](https://www.ashrae.org/file%20library/technical%20resources/standards%20and%20guidelines/standards%20actions/samar_1_2024.pdf)):
- Three new modular zone-humidity-limiting/dehumidification strategies (choose one, two, or all three).
- Outdoor air pollution mode — disables airside economizer above a user-defined contaminant (e.g., PM2.5/wildfire smoke) threshold.
- Fan staging addendum (fan-array staging) and laboratory VAV addendum (sash/face-velocity considerations).
- Corrections to multi-zone VAV AHUs with separate minimum OA dampers (fixing simultaneous OA/RA damper conflicts).
- Fan cycling during occupied-standby mode for fan-powered terminals and single-zone VAV AHUs.

## 2. VAV Terminal Unit Sequences

G36 covers these terminal configurations ([Efficiency Vermont G36 presentation](https://www.efficiencyvermont.com/Media/Default/bbd/2017/docs/presentations/efficiency-vermont-stehmeyer-napolitan-ashrae-guideline-36.pdf)):

- VAV, cooling only
- VAV with reheat
- Parallel fan-powered terminal, constant-volume fan
- Parallel fan-powered terminal, variable-volume fan
- Series fan-powered terminal
- Dual-duct terminal with inlet sensors
- Dual-duct terminal with discharge sensors

**Not covered:** Variable Volume and Temperature (VVT) systems. If the project uses VVT, write a conventional sequence — do not force-fit G36 language onto it.

### 2.1 Control architecture common to all G36 VAV boxes

- **Pressure-independent, cascade control**: an outer zone-temperature loop sets an airflow setpoint (cfm); an inner airflow loop modulates the damper to hold that flow regardless of upstream duct static pressure ([oaktrust.library.tamu.edu on cascade control](https://oaktrust.library.tamu.edu/server/api/core/bitstreams/04175478-867d-4f93-a6f3-5794ffea0244/content)). Requires **actual damper position feedback**, not just a commanded position — a direct hardware/cost implication for retrofit projects.
- **Discharge air temperature sensing at the VAV box** is required for the cascade heating-loop-drives-reheat, reheat-drives-damper (on a different scale) control used in VAV-with-reheat boxes.
- **Minimum airflow setpoint** = greater of: (a) code-required ventilation (62.1 people + area components, Voz — not an arbitrary % of cooling max), (b) heating-mode minimum sufficient to deliver reheat capacity without excess discharge temperature, (c) occupied-standby/unoccupied minimum.
- **Maximum airflow setpoint (cooling)** = mechanical design cooling maximum cfm.
- **Zone group membership**: every VAV box belongs to a zone group tied to an AHU/schedule; the box's local occupancy mode participates in the group-level mode logic (Section 4).

### 2.2 VAV, cooling only

**Occupied mode:**
- Zone temp loop (PI) modulates airflow setpoint between Vmin and Vcool-max as zone temp rises above cooling setpoint.
- Below cooling setpoint (in deadband or below), airflow setpoint holds at Vmin.
- Damper position loop (inner, faster PI) modulates damper to hold commanded airflow setpoint using the flow sensor/cross feedback.

**Unoccupied mode:**
- Damper closes to zero flow (or code-minimum unoccupied ventilation floor, if continuous ventilation is required) unless the zone is in Warm-up/Cooldown/Setback mode.

**Reset/requests generated by this box:**
- SAT reset request when airflow is at Vcool-max and zone temp still above cooling SP + a defined offset for a defined time.
- Static pressure request when damper ≥95% open or measured airflow <90% of setpoint.

### 2.3 VAV with reheat

**Occupied mode — cascaded three-stage logic:**
1. **Cooling stage**: zone temp above cooling SP → airflow setpoint rises from Vmin toward Vcool-max (same as cooling-only box); reheat valve/damper stays at 0%.
2. **Deadband**: airflow holds at Vmin; reheat stays at 0%.
3. **Heating stage**: zone temp below heating SP → airflow setpoint stays at Vmin (or rises to a defined heating-max if reheat capacity at Vmin is insufficient) while the **discharge air temperature loop** (cascaded from the zone heating-demand signal) modulates the reheat valve to maintain a calculated discharge temperature setpoint, itself bounded by a maximum discharge temperature limit (commonly SAT + 20–30°F, verify against reheat coil selection).

**Key numeric example to always state explicitly in the sequence:**
- Vmin, Vcool-max, Vheat-max (if different from Vmin) in cfm.
- Discharge air temperature max limit in °F.
- Zone temp deadband width (typically 2–5°F between occupied heat/cool SP, [ASHRAE 55-based deadband guidance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ASHRAE_55)).

**Leaking valve FDD (embed directly in the sequence, not just as an afterthought):** if the heating valve is commanded to 0% for 15 minutes and discharge air temp remains >3°C (5°F) above AHU SAT with the fan proven on, generate a Level 4 alarm ([NIST TN2024 on G36 VAV FDD](https://nvlpubs.nist.gov/nistpubs/TechnicalNotes/NIST.TN.2024.pdf)).

### 2.4 Parallel fan-powered terminal (constant-volume and variable-volume fan)

- Primary (cooling) air damper controlled the same as VAV-with-reheat cooling stage, drawing from the cold-deck/central AHU supply.
- Terminal fan (drawing plenum/return air across the reheat coil) starts when zone calls for heating (or per occupied-standby fan-cycling addendum, cycles briefly even without a heating call to maintain minimum air movement).
- **Constant-volume fan version**: fan runs at fixed speed whenever commanded on; heating output modulated by reheat valve only.
- **Variable-volume fan version**: fan speed itself modulates with heating demand, in addition to (or instead of) reheat valve modulation — state which strategy governs to avoid two loops fighting.
- 2024 addendum permits fan cycling during occupied-standby mode — confirm whether this option is adopted for the project.

### 2.5 Series fan-powered terminal

- Terminal fan runs continuously whenever the zone is occupied (constant volume through the terminal), drawing a blend of primary air and plenum/return air.
- Primary air damper modulates between Vmin and Vcool-max exactly as the cooling-only sequence; the terminal fan maintains constant total discharge airflow independent of primary damper position.
- Reheat valve modulates for heating calls, as in the VAV-with-reheat cascade.
- Because the terminal fan runs continuously in occupied mode, note explicitly whether it cycles off in unoccupied mode or continues running for continuous ventilation/circulation — state this, don't leave it implied.

### 2.6 Dual-duct terminal (inlet sensors vs. discharge sensors)

- Two primary airstreams (typically cold deck and hot/warm deck) each with a damper; a mixing sequence blends the two to hit either a discharge airflow setpoint (cooling-only-style cascade) or a discharge air temperature setpoint.
- **Inlet sensor version**: airflow sensors on each inlet duct; box logic sums/ratios inlet flows to hit total discharge airflow setpoint.
- **Discharge sensor version**: a single discharge airflow (and/or temperature) sensor downstream of the mixing point provides the feedback signal instead of two inlet sensors — fewer sensors, but the control loop must infer damper allocation without direct visibility into each duct's contribution.
- State explicitly which version is being used — the point list and FDD rules differ.

## 3. Multi-Zone VAV AHU Sequence (Airside G36)

### 3.1 Fan control
- Supply fan VFD controlled by duct static pressure T&R (Section 3.2 of SKILL.md) with the sensor located per Guideline 36 guidance — not right next to the fan, at a point representative of the ductwork's pressure drop, commonly ~75% down the longest/most critical run ([Addendum h to Guideline 36-2021](https://www.ashrae.org/file%20library/technical%20resources/standards%20and%20guidelines/standards%20addenda/g36_2021_h_20240229.pdf)).
- Return/relief fan (if present) tracks supply fan via building/duct pressure control to maintain building static pressure setpoint (commonly a small positive pressure, e.g., 0.02–0.05 in.w.g.).

### 3.2 Economizer and mixed-air control
- Economizer enabled when OAT (or OA enthalpy, if enthalpy economizer per 90.1 climate-zone requirement) is below the high-limit setpoint AND mechanical cooling would otherwise be required.
- OA damper modulates to maintain either a calculated mixed-air temperature setpoint or, in G36's minimum-OA-damper configuration, a separate minimum OA damper maintains a fixed/measured minimum ventilation airflow independent of the economizer damper — **do not let mixed-air-temperature-only control set the ventilation minimum**, a known 90.1-flagged legacy mistake (mixed-air temp control alone can't guarantee code-minimum ventilation).
- Mixed-air low-limit override: if mixed-air temp drops below the low-limit setpoint (typ. 45–50°F), OA damper closes toward minimum regardless of economizer logic, to protect the coil from freeze.

### 3.3 Supply air temperature reset (T&R)
- Requests generated by zones per Section 3.2 of SKILL.md (zone calling for cooling with reheat/damper fully closed/open at the appropriate extreme).
- SAT setpoint trims up (raises, saves energy) when requests ≤ ignore threshold; responds down when requests exceed threshold, within SPmin/SPmax bounds.
- SAT reset interacts with the humidity-limiting strategies (2024 addenda) — if adopted, SAT setpoint may be further limited/lowered to control zone humidity; state the interaction order explicitly (humidity limit overrides energy-driven SAT reset when active).

### 3.4 Zone group / occupancy mode logic
- Each zone group is in one of: **Occupied, Warm-up, Cooldown, Setback, Setup, Freeze-Protect Setback, Unoccupied**. The highest-priority mode among any zone in the group governs the whole group ([Yorkland G36 presentation](https://f.hubspotusercontent00.net/hubfs/8975906/ASHRAE%20Guideline%2036%20Presentation%20-%20Yorkland%20June%202021.pdf)).
- **Optimal Start**: pre-occupancy warm-up/cooldown timing computed from space temp deviation from setpoint, OAT, and (in more advanced implementations) historical building performance/thermal mass.
- AHU-level mode (e.g., does the AHU run during Setback mode for one zone group but not another it also serves) must be stated explicitly when one AHU serves multiple zone groups on different schedules.

## 4. Fault Detection and Diagnostics (FDD) — FC1–FC15 (Multi-Zone VAV AHU)

G36 embeds rule-based AFDD directly into the sequence, drawing in part from NIST AHU Performance Assessment Rules ([NIST TN2024 on commissioning G36 VAV sequences](https://nvlpubs.nist.gov/nistpubs/TechnicalNotes/NIST.TN.2024.pdf); [GOVPUB NIST summary of G36 FDD rules](https://www.scribd.com/document/582133631/GOVPUB-C13-386b4a80de7b30becf35a563468898e7)). The widely cited FC1–FC15 set for multi-zone VAV AHUs covers conditions such as:

| Fault # | Category | Example trigger logic (generalize to project's actual sensors) |
|---|---|---|
| FC1 | Duct static pressure control | Fan at 100% speed but static pressure below setpoint by a defined margin for a sustained period |
| FC2 | Mixed-air temperature too low given OA/RA temps | MAT falls outside the range implied by measured OAT/RAT and damper positions |
| FC3 | Mixed-air temperature too high given OA/RA temps | MAT exceeds the range implied by measured OAT/RAT and damper positions |
| FC4 | Excessive OA/RA damper cycling | OA/RA damper commands hunting/reversing beyond a defined frequency threshold |
| FC5 | Insufficient outdoor air | Calculated OA fraction below the minimum ventilation requirement while economizer is not in a minimum-OA-only state |
| FC6 | Excess outdoor air (economizer not shutting off when it should) | OA fraction above what's needed given OAT/mechanical cooling state |
| FC7 | SAT too low given AHU mode and heating/cooling command | Discharge air temp below setpoint by a defined margin while heating (or economizer) is fully commanded |
| FC8 | SAT too high given AHU mode and heating/cooling command | Discharge air temp above setpoint by a defined margin while cooling is fully commanded |
| FC9 | Outdoor air temperature too high for free cooling but economizer still active | Economizer damper open beyond minimum while OAT exceeds high-limit setpoint |
| FC10 | Outdoor air temperature too low for current mixed-air/discharge temp achieved | Discharge/mixed-air temp inconsistent with a very cold OAT and current damper position |
| FC11 | Excessive coil valve/damper cycling / hunting | Control loop output oscillating beyond an acceptable frequency/amplitude envelope |
| FC12 | SAT too low with cooling coil valve fully closed | Indicates a stuck-open valve, cross-connected piping, or failed sensor |
| FC13 | SAT too high with cooling coil valve fully open | Indicates undersized coil, low chilled-water flow/temperature, or fouled coil |
| FC14 | Cooling coil valve leak (0% command, temp drop still observed) | Discharge temp drop across the coil while valve is commanded closed |
| FC15 | Heating coil valve leak (0% command, temp rise still observed) | Discharge temp rise across the coil while valve is commanded closed (same pattern as the VAV-box leaking-reheat-valve rule in Section 2.3) |

Always adapt the exact trigger thresholds/time delays to the specific project's sensor set and equipment — the table above is a structure to follow, not literal code to copy without review.

### 4.1 Alarm suppression hierarchy

G36 mandates **hierarchical alarm suppression**: if an upstream/"source" fault exists (e.g., AHU fan failure), downstream/"load" alarms it would otherwise trigger (e.g., every VAV box on that AHU reporting low airflow) must be automatically suppressed to prevent alarm floods ([GOVPUB NIST summary](https://www.scribd.com/document/582133631/GOVPUB-C13-386b4a80de7b30becf35a563468898e7)). When writing or reviewing FDD/alarm logic:

- Identify the source-fault points that should suppress a defined set of downstream alarms (fan failure suppresses all served VAV low-airflow alarms; chiller plant failure suppresses all AHU high-SAT alarms).
- State the suppression scope explicitly (which downstream points, which alarm types) — don't leave "related alarms" undefined.
- Ensure suppressed alarms are still logged (for later diagnosis) even while suppressed from active operator notification — suppression should reduce noise, not destroy the record.
- Plan for **field tuning**: false positives and suppression-hierarchy tuning are a known G36 commissioning pain point; budget several weeks of trend review post-startup before FDD is turned over to O&M without excessive nuisance alarms ([ASHRAE Guideline 36 implementation challenges discussion](https://www.ashrae.org/file%20library/technical%20resources/standards%20and%20guidelines/standards%20actions/samar_1_2024.pdf)).

### 4.2 Commissioning/override requirements baked into G36

- All hardware points must be **overridable through the BAS** for commissioning purposes (G36 Section 5.1.11).
- Zone-group-level force/override commands (e.g., force all VAV boxes in a group closed to leak-test the AHU) are a required capability — plan the program structure and point list to support this, not just the narrative sequence.

## 5. Point List / Program Structure Implications

G36 sequences require substantially more hardwired and software points than legacy sequences: damper position feedback (not just command) at every VAV box, discharge air temperature at every VAV box, zone group tables, importance multipliers per zone, and request-hours accumulators per zone/system ([ASHRAE practical implications summary](https://www.ashrae.org/file%20library/technical%20resources/standards%20and%20guidelines/standards%20actions/samar_1_2024.pdf)). Capture this at bid/take-off time — it is a direct hardware and EIKON programming cost delta versus a legacy single-loop VAV sequence, not a detail to discover during submittal review.
